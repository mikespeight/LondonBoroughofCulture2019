Bent London vintage troublemakers Duckie take to the forest to celebrate the legacy of Leytonstone’s feisty homegal Fanny Craddock as post-war pop culture, style, food, gender politics and aspiration combine in this very queer celebration of a real British icon.

Deep in the forest, on an illlegitimate cruising ground, Duckie invite you to a taste of Fanny in this late night alfresco bacchanalian feast, performance salon and 1960s aspirational girl group danceteria. 50 drag queen Fannys duel with 50 drag king Johnnies in a massive dance spectacle choreographed by Lea Anderson; local moderne foodie market stalls tempt you with continental treats; in role as Fanny hostess Amy Lamé referees a live outdoor Ready Steady Cook; DJs Readers Wifes play the likes of Alma Cogan, Kathy Kirby and Brenda Lee; plus a post-midnight feminist revolution from Bird la Bird.

Produced by Duckie

Who's Involved

Duckie

Duckie create good nights out and performance clubs that bring communities together.

From their legendary 23-year weekly residency at the Royal Vauxhall Tavern, to winning Olivier awards at the Barbican, to their community projects like The Posh Club, Duckie are purveyors of progressive working class entertainment who mix live art and light entertainment. They combine vintage queer clubbing, LGBT heritage & social archeology & quirky performance art shows with myriad of socially engaged culture clubs and public outdoor spectacles.
www.duckie.co.uk
